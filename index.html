👀 Sleepy Cues to Watch

  • Yawning or stretching
  • Rubbing eyes or ears
  • Looking away or zoning out
  • Getting fussy or clingy
  • Clenching fists

Tip: Start your wind-down routine at the first sign — don't wait until baby is overtired.

🕐 Wake Windows

At 5 weeks, your baby can only handle about 45–60 minutes of awake time before needing to sleep again. This includes feeding time!

A rough cycle looks like:

  • Wake up → Feed (15–30 min)
  • Brief awake time (15–30 min)
  • Show sleepy cues → Start soothing
  • Sleep (30 min – 2 hours)
  • Repeat!

🌙 Night vs Day

  • During the day: keep lights on, normal noise, interact
  • At night: dim lights, quiet voices, minimal stimulation
  • Night feeds: keep it boring — low light, no talking, no eye contact
  • This helps baby learn day from night (it takes time!)

💤 Soothing Techniques

  • The 5 S's: Swaddle, Side/Stomach hold, Shush, Swing, Suck
  • White noise or shushing mimics the womb
  • Rhythmic motion (rocking, bouncing, car ride)
  • Skin-to-skin contact is deeply calming
  • A warm bath before bedtime can help

🛡️ Safe Sleep Reminders

  • Always place baby on their back to sleep
  • Use a firm, flat sleep surface
  • Keep the crib bare — no pillows, blankets, bumpers, or toys
  • Room-share (not bed-share) for the first 6 months
  • Keep the room comfortably cool (68–72°F / 20–22°C)
  • Offer a pacifier at sleep time (if breastfeeding is established)

💛 Remember

At 5 weeks, there is no schedule — and that's completely normal. Your baby is still adjusting to the world. Follow their cues, not the clock. It gets easier!
